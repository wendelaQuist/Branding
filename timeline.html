The story behind Red Bull

1987

Dietrich Mateschitz became inspired by functional drinks from the far East. He was working on the concept since 1984 and after 3 years the recipe, brand position, packaging, and marketing concept became reality. Red Bull was launched in Austria where the new product category was invented: energy drinks.

1988

Werner 'Grizzly' Grissmann had a vision about extreme relay-races containing four brutal physical tests: running up a mountain, paragliding, mountain biking, and kayaking. Out of this vision came Dolomitenmann. The motto of the unofficial World Champion extreme sport is:' When suffering is fun'.

2001

Red Bull creates the fastest ice-skating sport in the world. Crashed Ice took place in Stockholm and the athletes took place on the 300-meter-construction with only the information to get down as fast as possible.

2003

Besides making the lowest parachute jump ever, Felix Baumgartner was also the first person to fly over a 35 kilometres canal with the speed about 350 km/h. Afterwards Baumgartner told reporters: 'this is his biggest project jet, but there will be more don't worry.'

2004

Red Bull started Wings For Life for people with a spinal cord injury. They collect money with a race for everybody, professional or not. All the assembled money goes to spinal cord injury research which goal is to find a cure.

2012

Felix Baumgartner wrote history again by falling from the edge of space. Baumgartner jumped from 39km height and reached a top speed of 1342km/h. Felix admitted: 'When I was on the top of the world, I didn't think about breaking records anymore, only coming back alive.

2016

Max Verstappen was the youngest Grand-Prix winner ever. Verstappen was the only dutch man and won his first race for Red Bull Racing
